About Socotra:

Socotra, the hidden island.
It is a remote and stunningly unique island archipelago located between
the Indian Ocean and the Arabic see, belonging to Yemen.
Its long isolation from mainland Africa and the Arabian Peninsula has resulted
in an extraordinary level of biodiversity and endemism, leading to its nickname,
"the Galápagos of the Indian Ocean."

Socotra is adminidtratively part of Hadramout Governorate, and it's about 350 KM
away from Almukala city (the capital of the governorate), and it's total area is
about 3650 Square KM .

What makes Socotrians special is that they have a unique strange language
- language not accent - called the Socotrian language , while it's actually
one of the Hymarite Semitic languages.

Places To Visit:

Arhar Beach:

Sand dunes overlooking the Araabian Sea.

Hoaq Cave

It's a huge cave in height, width and depth at the top of a mountain.
from the last place the cars can reach, it takes you around two hours to reach there,
and in your way up you are going to find a lot of endemic plants. At the end, there is a fresh water lake inside the cave,
but to reach there you will have to walk for about one hour and half.

Ra'as Arsal

It's located in the western end of the island,
and it's one of the best places for fishing.

De Hamry Reserve:

It's the only place where fishing is forbidden,
and that's because of the beautiful and facinating
coral reefs and fish. Moreover, it's the dedicated
place for diving.
